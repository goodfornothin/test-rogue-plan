The weekly story

Every Tuesday & Wednesday

Two nights, one floor, the same idea both times: a beginner-friendly class at 7:30pm, then a free social from 8:30pm where nobody's watching the clock. Most of the room on any given night came on their own — solo is the default here, not the exception — and within the first ten minutes you'll have a partner, a laugh, and a reason to come back. Bring a friend for a fun night out, or come completely solo; either way you'll leave with new dance friends.

Rogue Bachata began with Oscar and Boadicea and a simple idea: dance nights built around listening, not performance. From the first class, we wanted a room where anyone could walk in alone and feel safe on the floor within minutes — that idea still shapes every Tuesday and Wednesday we run.

How we teach

The Rogue Principles

Six principles form the backbone of everything we teach — from your first beginner class to teacher training — all built on listening to your partner rather than performing at them.

  1. 01

    Rogue Body, Bond, Beyond

    Ground in yourself, listen to your partner, expand into the space

  2. 02

    Rogue Legato

    Your center of gravity travels on a continuous, unbroken path

  3. 03

    Rogue Pause, Presence, Pivot

    Stop the movement, find each other, then redirect together

  4. 04

    Rogue Bounce and Roll

    When bodies meet, rebound elastically or redirect

  5. 05

    Rogue Humility

    Surrender your movement when connection is lost; seek the partner before continuing

  6. 06

    Rogue Lazy

    Relaxed and lazy, yet in time—a paradox found in both music and dance

Good to know

Frequently Asked Questions

The short version: no partner needed, beginners and solo dancers welcome, £10 a class with a free social after.

Do I need a partner?

No — you don't need a partner for either night. We rotate partners throughout the class, so you'll dance with lots of different people and never sit out.

Is it okay to come completely alone?

Yes, more than okay — it's the norm. Most people on any given Tuesday or Wednesday came on their own. You'll be welcomed at the door, paired up in class, and dancing with new people within minutes.

What's the atmosphere like for total beginners?

Warm and unhurried. Both classes are taught from zero with no assumed experience, there's always a cluster of other first-timers in the room, and the free social afterwards is a low-pressure place to simply watch if you're not ready to jump in yet.

How much does it cost?

The class on both nights is £10, and the social dancing afterwards is completely free. There's no membership and nothing to commit to.

Where are the two nights?

Tuesdays are at Cafe Sol, 56 Clapham High Street, London SW4 7UL. Wednesdays are at Keystone Crescent, King's Cross, London N1 9DX — about two minutes' walk from King's Cross station.

What should I wear?

Comfortable clothes you can move in. Smooth or leather-soled shoes work best — avoid thick rubber-soled trainers if you can. Heels are optional, never required.

Do I need to book in advance?

Wednesdays at King's Cross can be booked ahead via the links on this page or paid at the door. Tuesdays at Cafe Sol are pay-on-the-door — just arrive a little before 7:30pm.

Do you offer private sessions or teacher training?

Yes. We run private and small-group sessions — bring two leaders and two followers to split the cost — and a structured teacher training pathway from paid training sessions through to supervised teaching roles.

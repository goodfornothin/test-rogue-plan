Beyond Wednesdays: Workshops, Private Lessons & Teacher Training

15 July 2026 · From Oscar — what we actually offer, beyond the weekly class

Most people only ever know us as Tuesdays at Cafe Sol and Wednesdays at Keystone Crescent. But that's actually just one of several ways to train with us — here's the full picture, straight from Oscar.

Two streams: principle and fun

The regular Tuesday and Wednesday nights are what Oscar calls the "fun side" — social dancing is a core part of it, not an afterthought. The other stream is workshops: one-hour sessions focused purely on Rogue principles — the underlying partner-dance technique, without the social framing. Workshops were actually how Rogue Bachata started, before the weekly nights took over most of the time and energy. They're coming back into the regular rotation now that the weekly nights need less day-to-day maintenance to run. If you've danced bachata — or any partner dance — for a while, this is where the real depth lives; write in if you want to be notified when the next one's on.

Private and small-group sessions

Individual sessions are available at rates that flex depending on what you need. The efficient option: bring friends. If you've got two leaders and two followers, you can organise a small-group session together — split the cost, still get real individual attention. Fully private sessions (just you, or you and a partner, with Oscar, Boadicea, or another instructor) go a level deeper — Oscar's clear that this is where people get the most in-depth understanding of the principles, regardless of who's in the room.

The research

This isn't just a nice-to-have upsell — one-to-one instruction has one of the strongest effect sizes in all of learning research. Benjamin Bloom's famous 1984 study, known as "the 2 sigma problem," found that students given individual tutoring performed, on average, two standard deviations better than students taught in a conventional group class — meaning the average tutored student outperformed 98% of the control group. Bloom himself noted individual tutoring at scale is too costly for most institutions to offer everyone, all the time — which is exactly why the small-group option exists: it's the practical middle ground between full group classes and full one-to-one attention.

Teacher training — in the pipeline

The newest addition: a pathway to train as a Rogue Bachata teacher. It's structured as a progression — you pay for training workshops (roughly £10–15 a session) where you learn the specific "dance language" and exercises Rogue Bachata teaches, then you start assisting in classes once you're ready, then supervised teaching, then eventually paid teaching roles as the school grows. Oscar's reasoning for charging even a small fee up front is deliberate: "people who pay a little bit of money are serious about it."

The research

Both halves of this structure have real backing. First, the small paid entry fee: behavioural research on commitment consistently finds that even modest financial stakes meaningfully increase follow-through — data from commitment-tracking platforms shows users who put money on the line are roughly 3x more likely to actually follow through on a goal than those who make a pledge alone, with no cost attached. Second, the assist-then-teach progression matches a well-documented model of skill transfer called "legitimate peripheral participation" — learning by starting on the edge of real practice with limited responsibility, then gradually taking on more as competence grows, rather than being thrown in fully qualified or kept purely theoretical until "ready." It's how most real-world apprenticeships have always worked, from tailoring to midwifery — and it's exactly the assist-then-teach path being built here.

The throughline across all of it: as Oscar puts it, "the more people qualify with the technique, the more we can grow, and the more you will get opportunities as well." Whichever path fits — social nights, deep-dive workshops, private attention, or training to teach — it's all one school.

Start with a Wednesday, then explore the rest whenever you're ready — no pressure, no fixed path.

In Conversation with Bo: Teaching Without a Script

23 July 2026 · A relaxed chat with Rogue Bachata co-founder Bo

We sat down with Bo — dancer, teacher, and co-founder of Rogue Bachata — for a loose, unscripted chat about what actually happens in our classes week to week. No prepared questions, just a conversation. Here's how it went.

On what keeps people coming back

Bo
"I think it's really friendly and welcoming for beginners. There's no pressure, and participants don't feel like they're worried about what they looked like."

On naming a move after whoever invents it

Bo
"Something that Oscar did in one of our first lessons that we've taken forward is that if someone in the class does something a bit different to what we're teaching, it's fun to incorporate it into the lesson — and then we call it their move. It's so-and-so's move. I hope that encourages people to dance how they want, in their own style."

On why there's no fixed set of moves

Bo
"The whole point of the way we're teaching is to not have a set script. Within dance and within social dancing, the goal is to not have set moves. Things are constantly evolving and changing — I don't feel like it's ever set."

On how the classes are actually structured

Bo
"At the beginning of the class we focus on the basics and give people time to work on that and try it with the music. Then we give a loose framework of steps, but within that we give them time to experiment — different holds, a few counts of eight to try out their own thing. We tell them: do your own thing."

On what surprises first-timers

Bo
"They come in for a Latin dance and they think they're going to get salsa — they think we're going to teach salsa. Some people are disappointed when they hear it's bachata instead. But then they end up loving it way more than they expected, and they get converted."

No script, no fixed moves, just a loose frame that leaves room to play — and apparently, a lot of salsa dancers who walk in skeptical and walk out converted. If that sounds like your kind of dance floor, come find us on a Tuesday or Wednesday. No partner needed.

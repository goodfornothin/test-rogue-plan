How we teach

The Rogue Principles

Six principles form the backbone of everything we teach — all built on listening to your partner rather than performing at them.

Rogue Body, Bond, Beyond

Ground in yourself, listen to your partner, expand into the space

Rogue Legato

Your center of gravity travels on a continuous, unbroken path

Rogue Pause, Presence, Pivot

Stop the movement, find each other, then redirect together

Rogue Bounce and Roll

When bodies meet, rebound elastically or redirect

Rogue Humility

Surrender your movement when connection is lost; seek the partner before continuing

Rogue Lazy

Relaxed and lazy, yet in time—a paradox found in both music and dance

Good to know

Frequently Asked Questions

The short version: no partner needed, beginners and solo dancers always welcome, £10 a class with a free social — details below.

Do I need a partner to come?

No — you don't need a partner, ever. Most people come on their own and partners rotate throughout the class, so you'll dance with lots of different people by the end of the night.

Is it okay to come completely alone?

Completely normal — most people do. Both venues are lively, well-lit, and full of regulars who make a point of saying hello to new faces. Our team is there from the start to introduce you around, and there's never any pressure to dance with anyone you'd rather not. You can also just watch for as long as you like.

What's the vibe like for total beginners?

Warm and unhurried. Every class is taught from zero, so there's always a cluster of first-timers in the room — you're never the only one. Partners rotate throughout, so you meet lots of people in a relaxed, no-judgement way, and the free social afterwards is entirely optional.

How much does it cost?

The class on both nights is £10, and the social dancing afterwards is completely free. No membership, nothing to commit to.

Where are the two venues?

Tuesdays are at Cafe Sol, 56 Clapham High Street, London SW4 7UL. Wednesdays are at Keystone Crescent, King's Cross, London N1 9DX — about two minutes' walk from King's Cross station.

What should I wear?

Comfortable clothes you can move in. Smooth or leather-soled shoes work best — avoid thick rubber-soled trainers if you can.

Do I need to book in advance?

Wednesdays at King's Cross can be booked online via the links on this page, or paid for at the door. Tuesdays at Cafe Sol are pay-on-the-door — just come along a little before 7:30pm.

Do you offer private lessons or performances?

Yes. We run private and small-group sessions — bring two leads and two follows to split the cost — plus a teacher training pathway and live opera-and-bachata performances for weddings, corporate events, and parties, from £500.

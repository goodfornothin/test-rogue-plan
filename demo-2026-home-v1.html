How we teach

The Rogue Principles

Six principles form the backbone of everything we teach — from your first beginner class to teacher training — all built on listening to your partner rather than performing at them.

Rogue Body, Bond, Beyond

Ground in yourself, listen to your partner, expand into the space

Rogue Legato

Your center of gravity travels on a continuous, unbroken path

Rogue Pause, Presence, Pivot

Stop the movement, find each other, then redirect together

Rogue Bounce and Roll

When bodies meet, rebound elastically or redirect

Rogue Humility

Surrender your movement when connection is lost; seek the partner before continuing

Rogue Lazy

Relaxed and lazy, yet in time—a paradox found in both music and dance

Good to know

Frequently Asked Questions

The short version: no partner needed, beginners welcome, £10 a class with free socials — details below.

Do I need a partner?

No — you don't need a partner. Most people come solo and we rotate partners throughout the class, so you'll meet new friends and dance with different people all evening.

Is it okay to come completely alone?

Yes, completely. In fact most people do. It's safe to come alone — you'll be welcomed straight into the group, no experience, no partner, no pressure, and you'll leave having met new friends.

What's the atmosphere like for beginners?

Warm and welcoming for solo attendees and complete beginners alike. There's no judgement, no clique, and no one is ever the only new person in the room — we make sure of that.

I'm a complete beginner — is that OK?

Yes. Both nights are beginner-friendly and no experience is needed. We teach from the ground up, and it's genuinely a safe, easy place to try something new for the first time.

How much does it cost?

The class on both nights is £10, and the social dancing afterwards is completely free. No membership, nothing to commit to.

Where are the two venues?

Tuesdays are at Cafe Sol, 56 Clapham High Street, London SW4 7UL. Wednesdays are at Keystone Crescent, King's Cross, London N1 9DX — a two-minute walk from King's Cross station.

What should I wear?

Comfortable clothes you can move in. Smooth or leather-soled shoes work best — avoid thick rubber-soled trainers if you can.

Do I need to book in advance?

Booking ahead is recommended for Wednesdays via the link on this page, though you can also pay at the door. Tuesdays at Cafe Sol are pay-on-the-door — just come along a little before 7:30pm.

What your guests will see

An Aria Begins

An aria begins. No announcement, no stage — just a trained operatic voice cutting through the room, unamplified if the space allows, and every conversation stops mid-sentence. Then, mid-phrase, the singer steps towards his partner, takes her hand, and the aria becomes a bachata. He keeps singing. They keep dancing. Two art forms your guests have almost certainly never seen together, delivered at once, by the same performer.

This isn't a singer with dancers behind him, or dancers to a recording. It's one act: a professional opera singer with more than twenty years of experience performing at the highest levels of theatre, singing live while leading a full bachata duet with his partner. The physical control it takes to sustain operatic phrasing through turns, dips and body movement is exactly what makes the room lean in — it looks impossible, and it's happening two metres away.

We are Oscar & Boadicea, the couple behind Rogue Bachata, London's bachata school — which means the dancing carries the same standard as the singing. The repertoire is shaped to your crowd, from full opera arias to popular crossover pieces, and can be adjusted in tone, language and length to fit the moment you want it to create.

Good to know

Frequently Asked Questions

The short version: from £500 for a multi-song set, London-based with travel quoted per event, and a repertoire we shape to your crowd — details below.

How much does it cost, and what affects the quote?

Performances start from £500 for a set of a few songs plus entertainment. The final quote depends on the venue, travel, set length and any add-ons such as a guest taster class or social-dance hosting.

Do you travel outside London?

We're based in London, where the from-£500 pricing applies. We're happy to travel further afield — travel beyond London is quoted per event.

Can the repertoire be adjusted for our event?

Yes. The repertoire is shaped to your crowd — from full opera arias to popular crossover pieces — and can be adjusted in tone, language and length to fit the moment you want it to create.

How long is the set?

The standard set is a few songs — roughly 15–25 minutes of performance, usually woven into the flow of your event rather than delivered as one solid block. Extended sets are available as an add-on.

How far in advance should we book, and is there a deposit?

Typical booking lead time is 2–4 weeks, though we can sometimes accommodate shorter notice. A deposit secures your date.

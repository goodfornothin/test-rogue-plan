Frequently Asked Questions

Everything people usually ask us before their first class or social — for anyone messaging us on Instagram, Facebook or WhatsApp.

Getting Started

Do I need a partner or any prior experience?

No, on both counts. We rotate partners throughout class, so you'll dance with lots of different people. Total beginners are welcome every single week — there's always someone at their first class alongside you.

What should I wear?

Comfortable clothes you can move in, and flat or low-heeled shoes with a smooth sole if possible (trainers with heavy grip can make turns harder). No need for dance shoes to start.

What actually happens in a class?

We start with the fundamentals — connection, basic steps and movement — in a friendly, low-pressure atmosphere, then move into exercises that build listening and lead-follow connection rather than just memorising a routine.

Beyond the Weekly Class

What is Rogue Resonance?

Rogue Resonance is our advanced workshop series, focused on technique and artistry beyond the weekly class — groundedness, flow and authentic connection, taught by Oscar.

Do you offer private lessons?

Yes — private and small-group sessions are available, including in-home couples sessions for those wanting to deepen connection and musicality together, on a weekly, bimonthly or monthly basis.

Is there a path to becoming a teacher with Rogue Bachata?

Yes. Teacher training runs inside Rogue Resonance workshops. From there, some dancers get the opportunity to assist at classes, and eventually to teach — which is when it becomes paid work.

I'm already an experienced dancer — is this still for me?

Yes, and we'd actually encourage you to drop into the open beginner classes too. Being able to lead or follow a beginner well, with care and patience, is itself an advanced skill.

Our Philosophy

What does "Rogue" actually mean?

It's about principles over constraints. Rather than arriving with a fixed set of steps to perform, you arrive as yourself and build the dance out of real listening and connection with your partner, in the moment.

Do you teach set routines?

Yes, alongside the improvisation work — routines are useful, especially since a different mix of people shows up every week. But the aim, even inside a taught pattern, is still to stay genuinely listening to your partner rather than just executing steps.

How do you get people comfortable with being spontaneous?

Through structured exercises rather than just telling people to "be spontaneous" — simple drills like step-tap, side to side, that build listening and connection into muscle memory so it carries over naturally into the social.

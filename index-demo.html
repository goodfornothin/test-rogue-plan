Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before your first dance

Do I need a partner to attend Rogue Bachata classes?

No partner needed at all. We rotate partners during classes so everyone gets to dance with different people. Just come on your own and you'll be paired up.

Is Rogue Bachata suitable for complete beginners?

Absolutely. Our Wednesday class at King's Cross is specifically designed for beginners — no experience required. You'll learn basic Bachata steps in a friendly, welcoming environment.

How much does Rogue Bachata cost?

The Wednesday beginners class at King's Cross is just £10. The Tuesday class at Cafe Sol Clapham is £10. Social dancing after both classes is completely free.

Where are Rogue Bachata classes held?

We run two weekly events: every Wednesday at Keystone Crescent, King's Cross, London — and every Tuesday at Cafe Sol, 56 Clapham High Street, London SW4 7UL.

What is Bachata?

Bachata is a partner dance from the Dominican Republic, danced to romantic Latin music. It's known for its sensual hip movements, close connection, and expressive styling. It's one of the easiest Latin dances to pick up, making it perfect for beginners.

What should I wear to a Bachata class?

Comfortable clothes you can move in — nothing special required. For shoes, anything with a smooth sole works well. Avoid trainers with thick rubber soles as they grip the floor too much. Heels are optional.

What is Rogue Bachata's style?

Rogue Bachata is a distinctive style developed by Oscar, combining his 20+ years of social dance experience with his background as a theatre performer. It focuses on authentic connection, groundedness, flow, and musical expression — known as Rogue Resonance.

Do I need to book in advance?

Booking is recommended for both classes (£10 each) — book Wednesdays at King's Cross via the Wednesday event page, and Tuesdays at Cafe Sol via the Tuesday event page. The free social dancing after both events is free and requires no booking.
